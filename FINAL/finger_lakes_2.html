Enviorment

Visitors of the Finger Lakes can experience all four seasons. Summer ranges from 75 to 85 degrees Fahrenheit. As it transforms into the fall the air turns crisp and the foliage is breathtaking. In the Winter snowfall covers the region by November averaging 67.3" per year which is lower than Rochester and Syracuse.

There are also wildlife that are special to this park. This includes:

  • Grassland birds - Henslow's sparrow, grasshopper sparrow, American kestrel, northern harrier and bobolink
  • Shrublands birds - yellow warbler, Eastern towhee, brown thrasher, yellow-billed cuckoo, and goldfinches
  • Forest birds - Northern goshawk, ruffed grouse, American woodcock, and scarlet tanager
  • White-tailed deer
  • Wood ducks
  • Eastern wild turkey
  • Beavers
  • Bobcats
  • Red foxes

Landscape

In New York State's only national forest, there are lands that are used for free-ranging cattle. The forest has 30 miles of interconnecting trails, gorges, ravines, pastures, and woodlands that any traveler is free to explore. The biomes that are present in this park is forestlands, shrublands, and grasslands.

The size of the park is approximately 16,212 acres of all types of land.

The closest town to the Finger Lakes National Park is Hector.
